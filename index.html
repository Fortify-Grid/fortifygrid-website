The Problem

U.S. electricity demand is skyrocketing, driven by AI data centers, reshored manufacturing, and the electrification of sectors such as transportation and HVAC. Meanwhile, renewables like solar and wind are replacing fossil-fuel-based baseload power, driven as much by commercial forces as the desire for decarbonization.

But there is a problem. The intermittency of renewables, compounded by extreme weather, is driving both higher demand and more unpredictability.

Add to this an aging, underfunded and strained grid, and we have the perfect storm: chronic supply uncertainty combined with unprecedented volatility in wholesale prices.

This is an increasing concern for electricity grids world-wide.

Storage Solution

Batteries are fast-responding assets that can act as both load and supply as needed, making them an indispensable asset class for aging grids as they manage increasing demand and supply volatility.

The U.S. market for Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) has seen explosive growth, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) exceeding 70% over the last five years. BESS has matured to attract tens of billions in investment, yet the sector remains in its early stages.

Despite the current capacity of <35GW, the market is projected to exceed 250GW in the next decade, with global storage capacity expected to surpass 2,000GW over 25 years.

Fundamental shifts in storage technology and deployment are anticipated during this period. FortifyGrid is well-positioned to become a leader in this space, with a focus on innovation, technical expertise, and modular, distributed assets.
